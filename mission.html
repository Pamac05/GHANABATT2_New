Mission Overview

The Ghana Armed Forces has a proud history of contributing to United Nations peacekeeping operations worldwide. The deployment of the Ghanaian Battalion (GHANBATT) to Abyei demonstrates Ghana's commitment to international peace and security.

GHANBATT operates under the United Nations Interim Security Force for Abyei (UNISFA), established by UN Security Council Resolution 1990 in June 2011. The mission's primary objective is to ensure security and protect civilians in the Abyei region, which has been a contested area between Sudan and South Sudan.

UNISFA Mandate

1

Protection of Civilians

Monitor and verify the redeployment of armed forces from the Abyei region and protect civilians under imminent threat of physical violence.

2

Security Provision

Provide security for humanitarian organizations and personnel, facilitate the delivery of humanitarian aid, and support safe return of displaced persons.

3

Monitoring and Investigation

Monitor and investigate allegations of human rights abuses and violations of international humanitarian law.

4

Mine Action Support

Strengthen capacity of mine action coordination center and facilitate voluntary disarmament of armed groups.

GHANBATT Objectives

Primary Objectives

  • Maintain ceasefire and prevent armed conflict
  • Protect civilian populations from violence
  • Monitor movement of armed forces
  • Support humanitarian operations
  • Facilitate peaceful resolution of disputes

Secondary Objectives

  • Build trust with local communities
  • Provide medical and educational support
  • Conduct civil-military cooperation activities
  • Support infrastructure development
  • Promote reconciliation and dialogue
